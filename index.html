A timeline of world-changing discoveries since the beginning of the century,
according to Chris Woodford at ExplainThatStuff!

A list of inventions between 2000 - 2019

This is a paraphrase and trim of the actual list by Chris Woodford, which actually starts about 4-5 billion years ago. I encourage you to check it out here.
Date Invention How I think it changed the world
2001 Enter: the iPod! The beginning of the end for CD's and DVD's started here! The innovation of mobile storage would never be the same after this.
2001 Wikipedia is born, thanks to Larry Sanger & Jimmy Wales! Nowadays, we can scour the Google for information, but if you ever wanted to find out everything about anything in one click, you'd end up here.
2002 iRobot releases Roomba® It's not lazy, it's smart! With the charge of a battery, you can let Roomba® pick up the dust you brought! Not recommended for real messes!
2004 An important year for electronic voting in the US. More votes were cast electronically than ever before, playing a huge role in deciding George Bush as the winner.
2004 Graphene is discovered by Andre Geim & Konstantin Novoselov. According to the site, this is a material made from sheets of carbon only 1 atom thick. A great example used by Woodford, is when you use a pencil. Graphites slowly peel off and get leave behind... a love letter you'd never send...
2007 Apple releases the first iPhone. The iMother-phone. The very first version of my current iPhone X revolutionizes the phone industry with a touchscreen, bidding other huge companies like Samsung to catch up.
2013 Elon Musk announces the "hyperloop."" Imagine an airless tunnel only accessible by trains moving at 750mph. The idea is to decrease friction by means of magnetic rails and an air-less, low pressure tunnel.
2015 Supercomputers revealed to be only 30x less powerful than human brains. I have to admit I'm surprised we over took them to begin with. Computers growing in power means that the future of complete automation is not far behind. What happens when computers are 30x more powerful than human brains?
2016 Nanotechnological Milestone Woodford captures the beauty of this moment with his own words, "Three nanotechnologists win the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for building miniature machines out of molecules."
2019 Google claims "quantum supremacy" With their invention of a "quantum computer," Google claims that it can compute faster than average one. Understanding the theory behind quantum mechanics is, for lack of a better term, insane. If this claim has credibility, I can only applaud it.
